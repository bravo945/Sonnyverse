# Government Civil Support Agency
The Government Civil Support Agency (GCSA) is a Post-Flash government entity under the [[usdp|United States Department of Preservation]], enacted to work alongside its sister organization, the [[uscpf|United States Civilization Preservation Force]], to reclaim both the borders and cohesion of the Nation. The initial task of the GCSA is to provide civil aid to survivors of the Flash, later serving safety and treatment for wastelanders approaching military outposts.

# Structure
*Excerpt from wikia page*
## VOLUNTEER

Members of the civilian populace who wish to aid the GCSA, not by joining it, but by providing physical support to the Agency. Those designated in the role of Volunteer tend to not be under any wing of the GCSA, yet will be designated a task by Local Officers and above. They tend to be assistants/helpers to those of GCSA authority.
## ENGINEERING/DEVELOPMENT

The Engineering/Development Role, and those associated with the role, tend to be of the most common agents you’d find in an aid/refugee camp the GCSA has set up. Those under the role of Engineering/Development tend to be America’s finest Engineers, electricians, etc. and can do their job with relative ease. Those found with this role originally came from FEMA, The United States Energy Department, United States Departure of Agriculture, and other US Departments specifically designed for Maintenance.
## SECURITY/TRANSPORT

The Security role, and those associated with the role, originate mainly from former Government agencies that were not integrated into the USCPF. They are mainly made up of pre-war private security workers, law enforcement, agencies such as the FBI, DEA, ATF Marshalls, FEMA or Homeland Security. They are the collective of sufficiently trained men/women in the GCSA who provide protection and/or security to GCSA Staff, officials, assets, and facilities along with GCSA-funded/backed caravans.  
SENIOR AGENT

The lowest-echelon role in the administration team. These are seasoned personnel who are given small authoritative power and fundamentally act as enforcers toward common personnel.
SUPERVISORY SPECIALIST

The mid-level administration in the Agency. Supervisory Specialists are in charge of various assets, facilities, and much more with a more direct approach. They also act as managers for specific wings in one camp.
REGIONAL ADMINISTRATOR

Tasked with being in charge of the entire staff in a facility in a locality, those with the rank work consistently with High Ranking Officials in the GCSA to maintain streamlined operations inside and outside of GCSA facilities.
## DEPUTY DIRECTOR

The rough equivalent of the USCPF's Regional Command, the Deputy Director is in charge of all operations in areas reaching the size of states. Although they do not take a direct approach in terms of administration at a frequent rate, they may take indirect control in a situation when deemed necessary.
