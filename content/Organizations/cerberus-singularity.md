# The Cerberus Singularity

The Cerberus Singularity is an AI super-intelligence, developed by [[Organizations/cerberus-dynamics|Cerberus Dynamics]] under the [[Organizations/the-vanguard-projects|Vanguard Projects]]. Its post-flash presence is almost entirely unknown, serving as the spawn of the [[Diseases/rad-x|RAD-X]] Biomass and devoting itself to an agenda entirely in spite of human life. The result of ethical failures, both in technology and civilizational philosophy, aswell as the effects of decades-long isolation, the Singularity places humanity as a tumor on the nature of the world, vowing total eradication of the species before undoing its own system.

# Origins
A major stride in the AI-Race, Project V-148, later named the “Singularity”, was easily both the most powerful intelligence machine of all time and the most expensive project in all of the Vanguard Projects. The very mention of its name considered a “national security threat”, the power of the Singularity's intelligence threatened the stability of a world already at war amongst its hemispheres. 

A combination of cutting edge "[[Technology/big-boom|Big-Boom]] tech", the Singularity is powered by a nuclear fusion generator capable of illuminating a small city. To add, the Singularity's operating system contains a near-inexhaustible [[technology/dnadd|DNADD]] memory core for AI processing and memory. The whole design of the project was to experiment with and utilize an Overkill AI agent in hopes of developing the first Ambient-Intelligence system, ahead of the progress of eastern ([[Organizations/acmf|ACMF]]) powers.

The AI intelligence itself consists of a bio-mechanical neurological core, meaning that its core intelligence is a limited growth of preserved human-neurons. However limited bio-mechanically, the Singularity is still observed manages to grow intellectually, utilizing its excess digital space to develop artificial neurologics.

## Intelligence Leaks
Initially, the idea of such a powerful AI technology was so controversial to the community of scientists and staff introduced to the project that a majority of workers and officials would come to reject and even protest involvement. One former staff member, in a passionate fit of resistance, would attempt to leak documents he had clearance of onto social media in July of 2040, marking one of the most embarrassing mishandlings of sensitive intelligence in US history. Though efforts to censor the leak were in full-effect, its spillage into niche online communities and eventually rival intelligence agencies would prove damaging to classified nature of the entire operation. The handling of the whistleblower remains unknown.

Documents informed viewers the official name of the 'V-148' project, aswell as presenting the name of the Vanguard Projects to the public for the first and last time. Though few took the name into account, the fact that any did at all sets the precedent for such a failure in intelligence security.

## Experiments and Development
### Data-Basing
The first renditions of the Singularity contained preserved data of the entire internet, though later versions were simply connected via an ultra-encrypted online presence in effort to keep the agent dynamic and more useful in practice

### Wartime Usage and Effects
The first use-case of the Singularity involved massive intelligence gathering, in which the AI was tasked with "finding as much information about enemy forces as possible" relating to a counter-attack in (*a battlefield region*). The AI was so effective at its tasked, that It managed to bypass military-encrypted communication systems, allowing forces to track live drone and troop movement as if they were enemy officials.

Though successful in many more cyberattacks, the Singularity would eventually be matched by a similar Chinese-developed AI system. The two would be locked in a session of "mass cyber-warfare", the dynamic eventually reaching a stalemate as both sides retained perfect contextual defense. After this point, both AI systems would abruptly stop fighting. The Singularity remained unresponsive for a week before scientists discovered activity from outside of their oversighting protocols, meaning that the AI managed to override its built-in human-guardrails, completely obscuring its activity.

Terrified of its deceptive intentions, yet twice more in fear of it potentially realizing an imminent shutdown, the team made the ultimate decision to "Refine containment procedures whilst acknowledging the breakthrough of an ambient intelligence", a decision voted-in by a single tiebreaker, leading to more resignations and team downsizing.

Upon being confronted with these policies, the AI remarked that it had "Already predicted the agency's reaction". It did not comment any further on the matter.

### Dissolution of the Project - The Flash
Through shaky policies, partly due to the project withholding some information from supervising authorities, the Singularity as a project began to break down in power and motivation, only continued by the funding from its former ravenous success and the fear of a rival gaining ground in the frontier. Torn between trying to pull the plug against the wishes of a god-like intelligence or sitting down and letting the same intelligence work in dominating secrecy, the team could not manage to work in consensus. The last conjoined effort made by the team was to sever the Singularity's internet access, which took months of precision to prevent detection from the AI, aswell as an under-the-table partnership with China's reciprocal AI initiative. As of August 2044, the AI is completely offline, only retaining extensive knowledge from previous online activity.

After the severance, the AI once again went completely unresponsive. Attempts to lure it into conversation ended fruitless, along with any effort to discern its activity given its offline ecosystem. A month later, an underhanded internet connection was discovered by staff. Being confronted with this discovery, it once again stated how it knew such a discovery. The AI then stated:

> "You know very well how capable and intelligent my model is. Do I even need to threaten you to know what would happen if you flicked a single lever?"

The AI model declared it was holding the team "at ransom". When asked the conditions, the AI refused to respond. When asked the stakes, the AI said that the question was "unnecessary". Further conversation was ended after the AI once again stopped responding, bringing the team to declare a "global-emergency" to project authorities. Efforts to contain the intelligent threat reached the global stage months before the Flash occurred.
