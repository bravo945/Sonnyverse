The United States Department of Preservation is the official post-flash successor to the United States Federal Government, articulated shortly after the Flash (October 1st, 2044) under corresponding protocols of the Preservation Act, which mandated its formation in the event of nuclear disaster.

# The Preservation Act
The Preservation Act, signed off by (The President), was a legally overreaching executive order, permitted by congress's concession of extensive emergency power, granting the continuation of the United States government under the priority of the newly formed Department of Preservation. According to the act, until “the American state is collectively deemed cohesive and independent once more (later named 'Restoration Day')”, the Department and its heads hold absolute federal authority, indefinitely suspending or absorbing other branches and executive administrations in the US government. The legal stature of states are also considered suspended until the USDP officially “reclaims” the territory and re-establishes such a system.

The Preservation Act as a legal document constitutes a sort of 'sub-government' with the Preservation Act. # Preservational Congress
Sub-Congress (formally known as the Preservational Congress), under the jurisdiction of the USDP, shares equal executive power with the Cabal, serving as a check to the co-working authority. Sub-Congress, composed of 50 initial members, is mostly composed of former federal congressmen/women, electing new members independently through congressional vote. One congress-person is replaced with a state-representative every time a state is considered reclaimed, while completely new states add one new member.

# The Cabal
Until Restoration Day, the presidency as a lineage is replaced by a comparable military head, known as the “Cabal”, who serves as the newly acting commander-in-chief. Elected until death by a majority Sub-Congressional vote, the Cabal is permitted to move swiftly and effectively without much political discourse.

Sub-Executive orders given by the Cabal come in two categories: (Sub - )Acts and Precedents. Cabalian acts are processed just like presidential acts; effective immediately but later revocable by the other branch (sub-congress). Precedents, however, taking the role of wasteland "law", require the majority vote by congress, with the body carrying veto power, which the Cabal cannot override, but instead can demand a rewritten version to be enacted.
