# Chicago Hobo Riots
> ~2036 - ~2037

The Chicago Hobo Riots were a period of city-wide unrest in regards to the government's tearing of homeless shanty-development throughout the [[chicago-underground|Chicago Underground]].

# "Super-Camps"
Over the former half of the 2030's, homeless communities began to form throughout the Chicago underground in response to a harsh local market, succumbing to AI and manufacturing automation, aswell as the city's lack of extensive support for the poverty line. Such a hostile economic climate pushed populations of various class, family, age, race, and gender, all onto city streets, overflowing the congregation of government-designated "tent cities" throughout former parks and urban-recreational spaces. Because policy for handling this growing epidemic was utterly inefficient, the dangers and lack of proper rationing within the tent cities led people toward the more historic congregation underground.

Migration into these tunnels would see a revival of the Chicago Underground, forming small shanty towns that vaguely resembled the the scrap-towns of the post-flash era. Media coverage of the shanty-towns would grow with population, with local and national eventually interpreting coverage, coining their name of "Super Camps" and attempting to raise concern and highlighting extensive drug-use within the camps. Shortly after its coverage in media, the police force would start ramping up efforts to dissipate and disband super-camps, giving camps a 1-day notice before rushing in and arresting all residents, who would usually face charges or short jail time.

# The Fulton Market District
The Fulton Market District was a Chicago district defined by its many headquarters of big-boom tech constructed along the decades of the pre-flash century, colloquially known as "the second Silicon Valley" for its market-dominance in the AI-Sphere.
## Fulton Hobo Tower
In the Fulton Market District sat an unfinished skyscraper from a failed contract for an AI corporation that went out of business mid-construction. The unfinished state of the building first enticed teens and content-creators before becoming a perfect hideout for homeless communities amongst the mass unemployment rates, eventually populating the tower and bestowing its name.
## Fulton Market Raids(2036)
In part of the series of Anti-'Super-Camp' campaigns by the government, law enforcement would eventually give the 1-day notice for the homeless population within the Fulton Hobo Tower, calling a near impossible exodus given the numbers throughout the floors. Camera footage depicting people racing down the floors were viral across social media, most notably a video of a man falling from the unwalled 19th floor from unknown conditions. Other outraging media of casualties from stampeding aswell as mass arrests of homeless persons would bring cities under protest nation-wide.
