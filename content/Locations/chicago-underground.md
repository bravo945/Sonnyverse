# Chicago Underground
> ~Early 1900's - ~Late 21st Century

The Chicago Underground is a collective of underground maintenance tunnels, sewers, and railways navigated and inhabited by a network survivors from the Chicago Flash.

# History
## Pre-Flash
The Chicago Underground, growing ever-faster in space by industrial and data infrastructure, aswell as in numbers from massive and abrupt unemployment rates brought by [[Technology/big-boom|big-boom]] automation, formed its first tunnels long before the Flash. Homeless unions throughout the underground walked abandoned railways and sewer tunnels in an effort to simply survive against the streets of a city that aimed to forget about them.

Though homeless characters of the earlier 2000's were more defined by epidemics and poor life choices, the underground 2030's saw a diverse generation of people simply unable to stay afloat in the economic flood of the time. This genuine population would contribute to forming sorts of 'shanty towns' throughout the underground, the most notable ones reaching virality around 2036, news stations coining the phenomena as "Super-Camps". That same year, a documentary about a super-camp located directly under the Fulton Market District, a sector of Chicago adopted by headquarters' of the major tech industry.
