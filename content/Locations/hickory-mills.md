Located at the southern edge of the Ozark Mountains in northwestern Arkansas, Hickory Mills is one of the largest and oldest towns in the Ozark Pact. The settlement was established in the mid 2040s by survivors looking to capitalize on the untouched forests in the area. Thanks to the state of the woodlands nearby, it would lead to the town's unique characteristic of being built out of crudely cut planks, as opposed to scrap metal like most post-flash scraptowns. Hickory Mills would join the Ozark Pact peacefully as they formed, being displeased by the CDF's failure to provide supplies for the logging operations taking place.

In the modern day, Hickory Mills is the Ozark Pact's, and the Mississippi River watershed's capital for lumber. The wood shipped out on the Arkansas River makes up almost half of the Ozark Pact's production of lumber, and provides materials for hundreds more scraptowns in the surrounding region.
