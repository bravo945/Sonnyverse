# Overview

The **Radiation Accelerated Disease**, commonly referred to as **RAD-X**, is a radiation-based disease that formed as a result of the [Flash](https://after-the-flash-official.fandom.com/wiki/The_Flash "The Flash"). RAD-X appears in many forms in the [After the Flash Series](https://after-the-flash-official.fandom.com/wiki/After_the_Flash_Series "After the Flash Series"), but the two strains responsible for creating the most abundant variants of mutants are RAD-XA and RAD-XB.

RAD-X as a personality is aggressive and predatory, preferring to risk infected and growth in full determination of expansion and conversion of humans.
***
# Background
## Origins
The origins of RAD-X trace back to 2032 in the weeks following the Flash. The few surviving wastelanders who lived on the irradiated surface, especially those near areas of concentrated gamma radiation such as impact sites or nuclear plants, found themselves as some of the first infected with the disease. Although there is no indication that there was a real patient zero of RAD-X, the survivors unfortunate enough to be the first generation were responsible for spreading the infection across the globe.

## Transmission
RAD-X is transmitted through direct contact with spores and infected cells. Food and water can also be used as a means of infection by being covered in spores or intermixed with infected blood. A Leacher spitting acidic barbs into an uninfected person's exposed tissue, a Sapien biting into someone's neck, or excessive amounts of inhaled spores are examples of transmission. The strain of mutant that infects someone doesn't impact what strain they will be, and the infected individual will progress through the stages normally depending on their exposure to biomass and whether they opt to use deradiators.

## Symptoms
One of the beginning symptoms, when compared to those not infected by RAD-X and those infected by RAD-X, is the change of skin pigmentation to a dull green or yellow. Subjects who display this change in skin color are often capable of transmitting the disease to other people.

Symptoms that closely resemble traditional pathogens occur, including coughing, sneezing, fatigue, vomiting, headaches, nausea, fever, and hallucinations. These symptoms will continue to worsen as subjects transition from the second stage of the disease to the fourth stage of the disease.

## Effects
The widespread transmission of the RAD-X disease has resulted in the destruction of many settlements attempting to build themselves back up and establish their position in their local economies by engaging in trade with other groups in the region, which was routinely disrupted with the spread of RAD-X. Wasteland settlements that have been devastated by the disease, whether it be through mass infection or mutant attacks, are known as Plague Towns, where the town is virtually empty of all human life and houses biomass that shelter those with RAD and is used to infect others. An example of a former Plague Town in the [Lake Mead Valley](https://after-the-flash-official.fandom.com/wiki/Lake_Mead_Valley "Lake Mead Valley") is [Craterview](https://after-the-flash-official.fandom.com/wiki/Craterview "Craterview"), a once-popular and thriving settlement that was completely overtaken by the biomass and experienced a biomass tree growth in its town center until all mutant life and traces were eliminated and the biomass tree broken down by [Southwest Libertarian Front](https://after-the-flash-official.fandom.com/wiki/Southwest_Libertarian_Front "Southwest Libertarian Front") CBRN specialists and the [United States Civilization Preservation Force](https://after-the-flash-official.fandom.com/wiki/United_States_Civilization_Preservation_Force "United States Civilization Preservation Force") in 2057.

# Initial Stages
The first three stages of RAD-X do not hold any significant physical additions to the host's body. These stages only apply to the RAD-XA and RAD-XB strains of the disease. These initial stages can also be sped through by steadily increasing the amount of radiation a host is exposed to as they transition from one stage to another, or can be altered through the use of deradiators

## RAD-X-1: "Incubation"
During the incubation period of the RAD, the disease is rendered noninfectious and can be cured by multiple deradiators upon immediate exposure. The incubation period can range from lasting for a few mere days up to a couple of months depending on how much radiation one is exposed to during this stage.

## RAD-X-2: "Yellow/Green Sickness"
Depending on the contracted strain, the host's skin color slowly transitions to a green or yellow pigment. The immune system is compromised and symptoms that resemble influenza begin to attack the respiratory system along with the neural system. Unlike the incubation stage, sickness is infectious and can be spread to others through direct contact with bodily fluids. However, the sickness can still be entirely cured with deradiators.

## RAD-X-3: "Refugee"
The skin pigmentation of the host continues to saturate into yellow and green hue. The symptoms from the sickness worsen as the host's mind begins to devolve into schizophrenia. Refugees are often irrationally paranoid and become extremely reclusive. RAD-3 marks the point of no return, where irreversible damage has already been done. Most refugees turn into RAD-4s, very few get the chance to turn into an Outcast.

## RAD-X-4: "Marauder"
The most versatile and abundant mutant in the wasteland, a Marauder's intelligence is retained but slowly begins to deteriorate as they evolve into a RAD-5. Alone, marauders continue even worsened symptoms from RAD-2 and RAD-3, but with other groups of mutants, their neural functions are hijacked to complete the objective of infecting others who have not been infected already and they continue to remain incredibly reclusive. Marauders act as footmen for organized biomass cells due to their ranged capabilities and large numbers, being able to fully handle firearms.

## RAD-X-5: "Sapien/Undead"
RAD-5s are not often seen outside large concentrations of radiation like Lake Mead Nuclear or the formerly Infected Craterview. On the rare occasion that they are, infected are often seen spreading spores and standing idly by nuclear waste as the disease controls their mind to seek out any source of radiation to bathe in. While nearby nuclear waste, Sapiens primarily function as guards for mutant dens and, unless a Marauder is present, have no hesitation when it comes to attacking uninfected. RAD-5 acts as a buffer stage between physically altered mutants and those in the initial stages. Those with the Yellow Strain transform into a RAD-6A while those infected with the Green Strain transform into a RAD-6B.

Additionally, RAD-5s also undergo a physical transformation as they further progress with the RAD-X disease. While these alterations force their body to take on an unsightly and ghastly appearance, their limbs are also vulnerable to detaching and their muscles will contort into unnatural positions. Sapiens will face detached limbs that expose their bone and tissue in their pathway to become a Creeper, and Chuckers will experience an accelerated muscle growth disproportionate to the rest of their body.

As RAD-5s are unable to feel any pain, Sapiens will use their exposed limbs to their advantage and attempt to wield them as personal blades that have the capability to spread the disease further.

# Strains
After Stage 5, infected mutants will develop mutations corresponding to their skin pigmentation.
## Yellow Strain (RAD-XA)

### RAD-X-6A: "Creeper"
Designated RAD-6A, Creepers are morbidly skeletal in appearance. They lack body fat, have inverted joints and a relatively low muscle mass, allowing them to traverse surfaces at impressive speeds and climb far better then most humans. Relatively adapted to dark corners and crevices, creepers carry better vision in the dark.

At this stage, mutants attempt to mimic cries from survivors in effort to lure in concerned individuals. Their attack pattern is then to snatch said individuals, either choosing to feed on their prey or render them unconscious, screaming bloodcurdlingly during the beating.

Though Creepers prefer a stalker attack pattern, their assertive and intimidating personality is still very apparent as a RAD-X mutant. 
## Green Strain (RAD-XB)

### RAD-X-6B: "Chucker"
RAD-6Bs retain their humanoid shape and the ability to communicate with other mutants, with the dominant arm of the infected mutant growing denser bones, compacted muscle mass, and hardened tissue. This asymmetric mutation means that Chuckers are slower than uninfected, but this is made up for in the utility of their newfound strength.

A Chucker's mutated arm is capable of hurling adult men, fracturing bones, and crashing through soft barricades such as doors and scrap walls. Disproportionate arm strength gives Chuckers the ability to charge into one direction at with a sudden burst of energy, grabbing those unlucky enough to be targeted and separating groups of armed survivors from each other. While they have the potential to charge at their victims, their speeds rarely match those of a survivor's sprint from their heavy muscle mass on their body.

# Biomass
Biomass are masses of organic, fungal-like tissue, fueled by warmth, radiation, and decomposed organisms. Biomass structures can grow indefinitely depending on their biotic factors and general intake.

In pursuit of absolute symbiosis with its infected agents, RAD-X biomass supports mutants in all kinds of ways; from sheltering, healing, accelerating mutation, to connecting all mutants under a structure to a general hivemind. The biomass serves as more than a structure, being a local network for the disease to consciously impose and improve itself over the general network.

Currently, the nearest notable biomass structure is growing along the nuclear crater in downtown Savannah.
