# Radiation Accelerated Disease - 'E-Strand'

RAD-E is an earlier strain of the RAD-X phenomenon, serving as a more primitive and unpredictable form of the virus mostly found and preserved in secluded areas. Sub-strains and mutations depend on the environment the associated biomass resides in, though most mutate subtle, adaptive adjustments. RAD-E is distinguishable from other forms of RAD in its more pack-like and decentralized structure, with its hosts usually becoming slender and agile.

![[Gallery/rad-e-infected.png]]

Though most information on RAD-E in the nuclear winter is hard to come by, the appearance and shift in habitat is a clear source of history for the strain. Most conclude that in earlier days, RAD-E defined itself from other vague strains by learning to grow in the dark. The moss of RAD-E is a blueish-grey color, appearing more like foamy moss than genuine vines. This moss can coat entire rooms, spreading quickly throughout halls with its faint color.

The spread of RAD-E is mostly airborne, though water likely can transmit the infection if too much is ingested. Transmission, if not from substance, is from contact with corpses and live infected. Victims of RAD-E first experience excessive coughing, and over a few days develop a fever-ish weakness that sends constant chills throughout the body, literally lowering one's temperature. As the virus prepares its incubation, the victim will eventually go comatose, after which will render them transitory. To this day, there is no cure after one collapses.

Likely judging from the state of the victim, the disease will choose to either consume the corpse or reignite it. if the body is unfortunate, one will reanimate under the possession of the virus. This new individual will have no memory of its previous life, seeking only connection with those under the same coating and carnage for those who aren't. Brutal in its attacks, individuals under RAD-E often mangle other victims, preventing them from even reanimating.

Though mutations diverge in nature, the most commonalities between each specimen is the pale, slender appearance -sometimes to the point of visible joints-  decaying hair if any is left, and constant frothing at the mouth. If seen, instances of RAD-E will either panic or enter a sort of fight-or-flight mode, choosing to flee more than most other strains. This is likely because each instance likes to attack their victim from a stealthy position, choosing to stalk the person before pouncing at a comfortable time.

The most interesting thing that deciphers RAD-E from other forms is its system of traits. Despite most strains' predictable paths of development, the "life" of a mutant under the disease revolves around adopting traits based on the ones observed by older mutants. Researches have seen packs of clawed mutants stemming from one mutant's effectiveness. In similar nature, the social structures of mutant packs also revolve around the longest surviving mutants, allowing the pack to learn from their leaders' attributes. This system is the main reason of why these mutants grow and adapt at such a fast rate.

# Beyond the Winter

after the clouds opened up, the numbers of RAD-E only lessened. Especially in places bound to warm temperatures, day time skirmishes and permanent areas were already a thing of the past, as infected learned to stay inside and away from the warmth. Not only disadvantaged by their illumophobia, interactions with other mutant strains would also challenge their infected, as most hiveminds didn't recognize the groups. Though smart, their weakness was no match for bigger forms that rose during the dustbowl era, and after reconstruction, these factors would combine with human re-innovation, marking the end of RAD-E in the east after a vaccine was created. Though adaptive, the feeble nature of RAD-E would doom it to the back-alleys and dark tunnels of rural North America.
