# Radiation Accelerated Disease - 'S-Strand'

RAD-S, colloquially named "the Biomoss" after its signature mossy biomatter, is a strand of the RAD disease found in the Louisiana Bayou, aswell as throughout the other bayous, marshes, and swamps along the Mississippi River. The disease seems to extend off of the [[Diseases/rad-x|X-Strand]], developing a much faster infection-rate and a more elaborate development process.

RAD-S has no hivemind or central core for its biomatter. Instead, the disease relies on a decentralized thick mossy-coating throughout a wetlands, keeping its hosts intelligent enough to tend to its system.
# Stages
## S-1: "Incubation"
- Immediate

*excerpt from RAD-X ATF Wiki Article*

During the incubation period of the RAD, the disease is rendered noninfectious and can be cured by multiple deradiators upon immediate exposure. The incubation period can range from lasting for a few mere days up to a couple of months depending on how much radiation one is exposed to during this stage.

## S-2: "Yellow/Green Sickness"
- 1-2 Weeks (Deprived of Radiation)

*excerpt from RAD-X ATF Wiki Article*

Depending on the contracted strain, the host's skin color slowly transitions to a green or yellow pigment. The immune system is compromised and symptoms that resemble influenza begin to attack the respiratory system along with the neural system. Unlike the incubation stage, sickness is infectious and can be spread to others through direct contact with bodily fluids. However, the sickness can still be entirely cured with deradiators.

## S-3: "Newbie"
- 2-3 Weeks (Deprived of Radiation)

The yellow/green pigmentation further saturates as the individual continues to mutate. At this stage is where the disease perverts the mind of its host, developing the necessary neurological components for mutation. It is at this stage where the infected person experiences the highest intensity in their fevers, rendering them completely bedridden for 7-10 hours. After this point, the person is considered lost, and the disease completely enveloped within its host.

## S-4: "Marauder"
- ~ 1 Month (Deprived of Radiation)

Marauders, being the youngest and most versatile mutant in the strand, wake up with immediate intentions to coordinate with other mutants and infect more people. The least mentally deteriorated, the mutant is able to have more agency in its action, even still lingual. However, the mutant is completely disinterested in complex or introspective thought.

Toward the end of this stage, marauders begin to develop conditioned dementia, completely forgetting former memories and relationships by the time they reach the fifth stage. 

This is the first and only stage that is able to handle firearms and complex weaponry.

## S-5: "Zombie"
- ~ 2 Months (Deprived of Radiation)

Zombies, nicknamed for their necrotic appearance and slow groans, are the most vulnerable and reclusive stage within the RAD-S system. Naturally obsessed with protecting and maintaining biomatter, if a Zombie isn't within the confines of a Biomoss growth, they are certainly trying to find one.

It is at this stage where a mutant develops corresponding traits depending on their sub-strand. A-Strand (Yellow) mutants develop wet and soggy skin, adapted for movement within moss growths, while B-Stand (Green) mutants develop dry and leathery skin, to the point of psuedo-scalage, allowing for biomatter to take hold on the skin of the mutant.

> Further stages require significant exposure to radiation and/or biomatter.

## (Yellow) A-Strain
### S-6A: "Mudman"
Mudmen are fast-moving mutants, adapted to wetland terrain. The stage is notable for the mutant's preferred movement on all-fours, even to engage victims. Mudmen have been seen away from wetlands, but do prefer their more familiar region.

It is at this stage where a mutant begins to develop light webbing on their hands and feet, making their grip slightly sticky.

### S-7A: "Shambler"
Shamblers are loud and aggressive mutants, naturally enraged, and not afraid to reach for victims out of the water. Unlike their earlier form, Shamblers tend to leave their swamp at night, actively looking to encounter victims to leap at in a violent rage. Such a rage is the main reason for such aggression, as the mutant stands constantly irritable and sensitive to their surroundings, which even leads to rare conflicts with other mutants that annoy them enough.

Adapted to both bipedal and quadrupedal movement, the Shambler is also vertically-agile, able to climb poles and grip-able surfaces with their hands and feet, albeit a little slow.

### S-8A: "Stalker"
The final stage of the A-Strain, a Stalker has mastered its environment, becoming both a respected leader of the growth it resides in and an urban legend among the uninfected. Learning to manage its inherent anger, the Stalker is the only mutant that can properly "calm" confronted Shamblers, incentivizing them to follow behind the mutant, creating a sort of pack dynamic. The mastery of an S-8A is unique in that one Shambler cannot just radiate itself into this final stage, rather it takes time, experience, and observation of another Stalker.

The silence and watchfulness of a Stalker does not mean it is at all afraid or unengaging. A Stalker is much the opposite, using its time and analysis to find a sophisticated and more effective plan of infection than the Shambler's familiar blind rage. From this calculation, many groups of wastelanders - even armed - have fallen victim to a skilled Stalker, infecting (or killing) impressive swathes of people at once. From stories of survivors, the presence of a Stalker has become urban legend, subjecting many either in disbelief or in complete fear of the night.

## (Green) B-Strain
### S-6B: "Golem"
Golems, colloquially named after their slow and bulky appearance, are the "protectors" of the growth they reside in. With hardened, leathery skin, sometimes protruding their own Biomoss, Golems carry a bigger frame, trudging through the perimeters of the wet environment they find themselves in, constantly looking for threats to engage.

#### "Wanderers" or "Green-Bears"
Rare instances of S-6B that do not claim a Biomoss growth are known to wander aimlessly without preference. Skittish and defensive during the day, the Wanderer only seems to attack when threatened or hungry.

The interesting aspect of Wanderers is how both themselves and their associated strain of mutants do not recognize each other, with other growth-based mutants viewing Wanderers as neutral lifeforms at best, threats at worst. Wanderers don't seem interested in infection, nor are they themselves infectious. They seem to carry no objective of their own besides the changing of scenery as they trudge in an awkward biological limbo; a legendary symbol RAD-Isolation.

### S-7B: "Shield"
Shields are bigger mutants notable for significant Biomoss growth along their body, resembling a crusher. Unlike the plates of the XB-Strain, though, the mossy growth on their bodies is more spongey, requiring the mutant to keep it wet every couple hours or so.

Shields make up for their lack of agility in their sudden and absurd gigantism, seeming to be symbiotically aided by their growth, both in nutrients and even in oxygen once totally ingrown. The surfaces of their bodies are covered in Biomoss, rendering blunt melee and small firearms near useless, more vulnerable to sharper and heavier firepower. Their balance and towering stature act as both cover for lower-stage mutants and a physical vantage for yellow-strained mutants, making them an effective tool in RAD-S combat.

Because of their excess growth, any exposure of garment or breath in the near 3-yard vicinity of a Shield would immediately begin infection.

### "Obelisk"
The Biomoss growth on an Obelisk is so excessive that the host can barely move, nor be damaged by conventional weaponry. The last movement of an Obelisk, called a "pilgrimage" is when one travels as far into uninfected wetlands as it possibly can before collapsing. After collapsing, the growth on the Obelisk completely consumes its host, beginning to spread rapidly around its new area until a new growth is established. This manually continues the cycle of the RAD-S culture.

# Vulnerabilities
Though one of the strongest and most elaborate strands in the RAD family, RAD-S as a moss is learned to be susceptible to:
- Fire
	- Most effective counter against RAD-S and its Biomoss
	- Burned regions are inhospitable for RAD-S
- Sharp Melee
	- Moss is easily broken by machete and sharp swinging weapons
- Herbicides
	- Some herbicides are found to slowly erode Biomoss on surfaces
	- The "RAD-icide" gas, later developed with the same effective substances in De-RAD by the GCSA during the reconstruction era, are also highly effective at killings growths.
