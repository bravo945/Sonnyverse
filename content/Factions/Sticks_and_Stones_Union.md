The Sticks and Stones Union (or SNS for short) is a group of survivors in the Chicago underground that originate from a union of homeless people who traveled the area before the flash.

###### Field Symbol for the SNS![[SSU has been here.png]]


# Introduction
Originally a plethora of hobos using the underground areas for safety and opportunity, the flash would force the inhabitants into a chaotic frenzy, fighting tooth and nail daily for crumbs and droplets. As food and drink became even scarcer, with even more people seeking refuge from the surface, each day would bring heaps of bodies succumbing to the dirty crowds.

The tunnels remained a wild hellhole until the city surface was habitable again, giving many an opportunity to find more food. This extension would release many from the overflowing depths of the tunnels, calling many more to use findings from the surface to trade with those who couldn't ascend.

Over time, the people that remained underground would restructure themselves around this symbiotic relationship with scavengers, designating members to rise up in groups to reap supplies, coming back down to share and sell with the rest of the group. The survivors, stained by the horrors of previous disarray, yet united under the same nature and system, turned to each other for warmth in the endless cold.

This would lead to groups like the Stick and Stones Union, sprouting from a band of hobos that garnered attention and refuge for their community’s efficient and familiar use of the tunnels.
# The Union

Calling the place home long before the Flash, the SNS had intricate knowledge of the sewers and the metro, familiar with resources, hiding spots, and other valuable niches a survivalist could need. Using their knowledge, the union would have an edge over non-native competition, selling information and valuable commodities while taxing and even robbing unfamiliar faces.

Though mostly unstructured, the community of the SNS takes pride in its small numbers, thinning itself across the underground with scavs and scouts to the point where no one in the Chicago underground is far from its presence.
