# Ozark Pact (Ozark Confederacy)
![[ozark-pact-overview]]

The Ozark Pact is a sparse coalition of settlements throughout the Ozark Mountain region, aswell as surrounding mainly the Springfield and St. Louis territories. In its prime, the faction held a expansive strip of Interstate-44, supervising trade and transport throughout its home region.

# History
## Formation (~September 2044)
Its name originally pointing to the city rather than the region, the Ozark Pact was born as an agreement amongst surviving communities and homesteads native to the cities of Ozark and Nixa, protecting themselves against threats from the incoming traffic of survivors from the flash of Springfield, Missouri. In such agreement, settlements recognizing the pact donate service, material, and militiamen to struggling areas facing flocks of refugees in reciprocity come the event of their own struggle.

The Ozark Pact eventually expanded its scope of refugee-centric deterrence to general aid against the freezing nuclear winter, which nearly collapsed the entire network, freezing numerous settlements to their peril while forcing many more toward stabler shelters. This amassment slowly transformed the faction from a small collective treaty organization into a centralized set of shanty-towns orbiting its home city, placing the Ozark Pact in a very ideal position as the caravan-economy took form.

## Expansion & CDF Annexation (~2048 - ~2052)
*Ozark Pact takes I44 leading to St Louis, encircling area but facing tribulations with native factions. Inner turmoil as faction reorients from native settlement protection to treaty-based conquest. CDF reaches region, turning disgrunted OP scrap towns under their militia, angering the pact and raising tensions until CDF proposes a trade agreement; OP keeps autonomy as a representative under CDF economic diplomacy given their own service material & men. From then on, OP supervises Ozarkan CDF borders, loosely claiming most of southern Missouri and outer St. Louis / Mississippi River trade settlements*
*also mention [[hickory-mills]]*

Once the clouds broke over Missouri lines, the Ozark Pact was granted a chance to breathe and warm from the winter, finding their surviving stature more fortunate than their barely functioning peers. In terms of organization around the Springfield area, the pact stood as the sole-lasting figure of order; a decentralized network of volunteers, merchants, and contractors, all set to reinforce the Ozark community. Naturally, this dominating position would only grow the faction, reaching neighboring towns that sought the same promise of mutual support. At this time, the regional caravan economy would come to lean on Ozark's diplomatic hands. 

### Gutting of I-44 
Interstate 44, once an unending path of frozen demise, eventually warmed into the transport and economic lifeblood of the entire Missouri state. Though the highway was valuable and well-traveled, its danger was just as prominent and limiting for the region. Nicknamed "the Missouri jungle" by locals, bandits throughout the route took advantage of the many vehicles littered along the highway to surprise victims and enemy factions. The highway banditry became such a successful venture that a culture of "road-runners" or small gangs living semi-nomadically along the highway, would come to call it home, making livelihoods on ransacking passerby's. Firefights were common amongst these road-runners and their complicated territories, engulfing the strip in near constant gang warfare, ensuring grim odds to anyone stationary for too long.

The danger and vehicle-inaccessibility of Interstate 44 only became a more relevant matter for the growing Ozark Pact. To the Pact, the highway was a forming frontier that tightened their grip over the mountains with every inch of held territory. Thus, it wasn't long before a full initiative of "civilizing the interstate" took form, a pipe-dream project at best that asked for piles of men, resources, and development from a barely recovering community budget. Though most people involved in the merchant trade supported or understood the project and its necessities, the intense taxing was barely justified in its controversy among the settlements and their behalved representatives.

Before the Pact could afford to scrap cars along the highway, the project was essentially a giant game of "whack-a-mole" against road-runners, who proved to the faction the savagery and depravity of urban apocalypse. Being mostly composed of city refugees, many rejected by the Ozark's walls, the road-runners had another layer of vengeance to their attacks, many aiming to unify against their common "colonial" threat.

Though rough and rigorous the first few years of the project, the brutality of highway forces served a sort of "shock therapy" to the unhardened militias of the pact, conditioning them to a proper invasive battlefield that would be useful in later fronts. The effort also was not left unrecieved, as many groups and caravans associated with the highway would praise and even support the initiative themselves, settlements joining the Pact as militiamen paved their span of the highway. As more hands joined the project, a system of scouting, clearing, and recruiting began to take shape; militiamen established camps and posts along the highway that welcomed travelers and offered protection to caravans (taxing them on refusal). these supervised caravans would allow troops to scout and document the area aswell as spread the Ozark branding down the way. The Pact would constrict stretches of road between two posts, emptying areas as scouting platoons moved through, keeping paths clean and ambiently guarded without constant armored supervision.

As the posts along I-44 truly began to clear areas, the construction-vehicle imports from [[hickory-mills|Hickory Mills]] would allow teams to knock cars off the sides of the road. By the end of 2051, more than half of I-44, Springfield to St. Louis, was drivable, and nearly the entire expanse claimed by Ozark Troops. By then, The pact had solidified its new territories, both along the highway and the annexed settlements around it, turning a contract between two cities into counties of interdependent communities.

### CDF Confrontation & CDF Annexation
The transition to such an expansive front was not easy. The amount of lives, taxes, and attention toward a frontier insignificant to the original members that cultivated the Pact left a bad taste among many. To top it all off, the legal system built to protect and represent the cities of Ozark and Nixa now left them a minority seat in their own government. The Ozarkan contract left no hierarchy or authority beyond the equal vote among each settlement, meaning the newest, barely-cities of the east had the same commanding power as the communities that founded the state.

The former patience of settlements among political troubles had no excuse after the annexation of the highway, without outrage tensing the bonds of each community as legal scandals piled up. To everyone in the system, it was clear that the structure of the pact was outdated, and had no business running the expanse of a pseudo-nation.

## Confederacy & USCPF Confrontation (August 1st, 2053 - ~Late 2070's)
*St. Louis declared capital, "new I-44" paved (designated road to the side of the highway), & major reconstruction to faction. USCPF imposes itself over the region and river after CDF presence is severed. OP fights with USCPF chapter, Called "Ozark-Federal War". Cohesive warring is rare over decade of small confrontations and firefights. OP remains independent and autonomous for the next couple of decades.*

# Structure
## Early Pact
### Settlement Representatives
*Settlement decides figureheads to conference with city committee expressing needs and abilities*
### City Representative
*chief of city committee. Manages settlement exchanges and convenes with other city representative(s) for broader conference.*
## Confederacy
### Precincts
*Metro Areas and settlements divided into Precincts*
### Precinct Representatives
*do not (have to) contain settlement representatives. Elected by settlement-coordinated decision (usually ballot)*
### County Representatives
*divided based on 3-4 collective precincts*
### National Committee
*run the military. situated in St. Louis. Modeled after CDF pseudo-government*
### Military
*official servicemen directed by the national committee instead of settlement-based exchanges and initiatives. held a draft during the Ozark-Federal war.*
## Militia
*loose armed forces protecting settlements and caravans. Ran by regiments, companies, and local support.*
