# Ozark Pact

>![[Gallery/ozark-pact-flag.png]]
>*Merchant Flag of the Ozark Pact*

The Ozark Pact is a sparse coalition of settlements around the Throughout the Ozark Mountain region, aswell as surrounding mainly the Springfield and St. Louis territories. In its prime, the faction held a expansive strip of Interstate-44, supervising trade and transport throughout its home region.

# History
## Formation (~September 2044)
Its name originally pointing to the city rather than the region, the Ozark Pact was born as an agreement amongst surviving communities and homesteads native to the cities of Ozark and Nixa, protecting themselves against threats from the incoming traffic of survivors from the flash of Springfield, Missouri. In such agreement, settlements recognizing the pact donate service, material, and militiamen to struggling areas facing flocks of refugees in reciprocity come the event of their own struggle.

The Ozark Pact eventually expanded its scope of refugee-centric deterrence to general aid against the freezing nuclear winter, which nearly collapsed the entire network, freezing numerous settlements to their peril while forcing many more toward stabler shelters. This amassment slowly transformed the faction from a small collective treaty organization into a centralized set of shanty-towns orbiting its home city, placing the Ozark Pact in a very ideal position as the caravan-economy took form.

## Expansion & CDF Annexation (~2048 - ~2052)
*Ozark Pact takes I44 leading to St Louis, encircling area but facing tribulations with native factions. Inner turmoil as faction reorients from native settlement protection to treaty-based conquest. CDF reaches region, turning disgrunted OP scrap towns under their militia, angering the pact and raising tensions until CDF proposes a trade agreement; OP keeps autonomy as a representative under CDF economic diplomacy given their own service material & men. From then on, OP supervises Ozarkan CDF borders, loosely claiming most of southern Missouri and outer St. Louis / Mississippi River trade settlements

also mention [[hickory-mills]]*

## CDF Rebellion (August 1st, 2053 - February 15th, 2056)
*local conflicts + lack of imports from broader CDF raise tensions. USCPF enter St. Louis under Reclamation Initiative, do not recognize OP. Small conflict, broader CDF enter informal treaty with USCPF, recognizing their claims along important St. Louis areas. OP protests, uses distant rebellions as chance to secede (Ozark Confederacy Constitution).*

*fighting is brutal and lines barely move. CDF blows up I44, crippling trade. Republic nearly dissolves before central CDF structure collapses, retreating and deserting forces, leaving inner CDF loyalists to fend for themselves.*

## Confederacy & USCPF Confrontation (August 1st, 2053 - ~Late 2070's)
*St. Louis declared capital, "new I-44" paved (designated road to the side of the highway), & major reconstruction to faction. USCPF imposes itself over the region and river after CDF presence is severed. OP fights with USCPF chapter, Called "Ozark-Federal War". Cohesive warring is rare over decade of small confrontations and firefights. OP remains independent and autonomous for the next couple of decades.*

# Structure
## Early Pact
### Settlement Representatives
*Settlement decides figureheads to conference with city committee expressing needs and abilities*
### City Representative
*chief of city committee. Manages settlement exchanges and convenes with other city representative(s) for broader conference.*
## Confederacy
### Precincts
*Metro Areas and settlements divided into Precincts*
### Precinct Representatives
*do not (have to) contain settlement representatives. Elected by settlement-coordinated decision (usually ballot)*
### County Representatives
*divided based on 3-4 collective precincts*
### National Committee
*run the military. situated in St. Louis. Modeled after CDF pseudo-government*
### Military
*official servicemen directed by the national committee instead of settlement-based exchanges and initiatives. held a draft during the Ozark-Federal war.*
## Militia
*loose armed forces protecting settlements and caravans. Ran by regiments, companies, and local support.*
